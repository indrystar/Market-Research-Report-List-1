<p><p>Fuses for electrical equipment and electronic products are categorized into four main types: filamentous, flake, glass tube, and ceramic tube. Filamentous fuses are made of a thin wire that melts when excessive current flows through it, interrupting the circuit. Flake fuses consist of a small metal plate that bends or breaks under high current. Glass tube fuses, as the name suggests, are enclosed in a glass tube and contain a wire that melts when overloaded. Ceramic tube fuses have a similar design but are made of ceramic material for enhanced durability and performance.</p></p>
