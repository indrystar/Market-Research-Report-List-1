<p><p>Wireless power transmission systems offer various types of technology for transferring electrical energy without the need for physical wires. Inductive coupling is a common method that uses a transmitter coil to create a magnetic field, which induces a voltage in a receiver coil. Resonant inductive coupling enhances this technology by utilizing resonant circuits to improve energy transfer efficiency. Capacitive coupling involves the use of electric fields to transfer power between the transmitter and receiver. Lastly, other types of wireless power transmission systems may include methods such as radio frequency and laser-based technologies.</p></p>
