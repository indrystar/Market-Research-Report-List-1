<p><p>Graphic Processors, or GPUs, are essential components used in computers and mobile devices to handle graphics-related tasks. They can be classified into two main types: discrete and integrated. Discrete GPUs are separate standalone units that can be added to a computer system to enhance its graphics processing capabilities. Integrated GPUs, on the other hand, are built into the computer's main processor, sharing system resources and providing basic graphical functionalities. Discrete GPUs are more powerful and suitable for demanding tasks, such as gaming or graphic design, while integrated GPUs offer lower performance but are cost-effective for everyday use.</p></p>
