<p><p>Temperature signal conditioners are devices used to convert raw temperature signals into easily measurable and readable output signals. There are two main types: current output signal and voltage output signal. Current output signal conditioners generate a current proportional to the temperature being measured, while voltage output signal conditioners produce a voltage signal that varies according to temperature. Both types are widely used in various industries such as manufacturing, automotive, and aerospace, providing accurate and reliable temperature measurement and control for improved process efficiency and safety.</p></p>
