<p><p>Marine aids to navigation are crucial for maritime safety and include various types. Lighthouses are tall structures with bright lights, guiding vessels at night or in poor visibility. Buoys are floating devices that mark channels or hazards, with different shapes and colors conveying specific information. Fog signals produce distinct sounds to warn ships during foggy conditions. Day beacons are fixed markers often used for navigation during daylight. Lastly, other aids can include range lights, lighted buoys, and radar reflectors, among others, all serving to support vessel navigation and prevent accidents at sea.</p></p>
