<p><p>OLED stands for Organic Light-Emitting Diode. It is a display technology that utilizes thin organic materials to emit light when an electric current is applied. OLED technology is widely used in applications such as smartphones, televisions, automotive displays, wearable devices, and lighting solutions.</p><p>The OLED market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. <p><p>OLED, or Organic Light Emitting Diode, is a display technology categorized into two main market types, PMOLED and AMOLED. PMOLED, or Passive Matrix OLED, is a simpler and less costly technology suited for small screens with limited resolution and color accuracy, often found in simple devices like wearable gadgets. In contrast, AMOLED, or Active Matrix OLED, is a more advanced and higher-quality technology primarily used in smartphones and high-end TVs, offering better image quality, color accuracy, and larger screen sizes.</p></p>
