<p><p>VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds) meters are used to measure the concentration of harmful gases in the air. There are two common types of VOC meters in the market. The first type is PID (Photoionization Detector) meters, which use ultraviolet light to ionize VOCs and measure their concentration. The second type is Metal-oxide Semiconductor (MOS) meters, which utilize a semiconductor sensor to detect and measure VOCs. Both types serve the purpose of ensuring air quality and safety in various industries such as manufacturing, construction, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
