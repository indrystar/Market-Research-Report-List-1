<p><p>Epigenomics refers to the study of changes in gene expression or cellular phenotype that do not involve alterations in the underlying DNA sequence. These changes are primarily influenced by various external factors such as lifestyle, environmental exposures, and aging, and can have a significant impact on an individual's health and disease susceptibility. The field of epigenomics has gained increased attention in recent years due to its potential application in personalized medicine and disease diagnosis.</p><p>The Epigenomic Market is expected to experience substantial growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neurological disorders is one of the key factors driving market growth. Epigenomic studies can provide valuable insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying these diseases, aiding in their early detection and developing targeted therapies.</p><p>Moreover, the rising adoption of epigenomic research in drug discovery and development processes is also contributing to market expansion. Epigenomic profiling techniques are being increasingly used in drug screening, toxicity testing, and assessing drug efficacy, leading to improved drug development pipelines.</p><p>In terms of technology, DNA methylation analysis is currently the dominant segment in the market. DNA methylation is an essential epigenetic modification and plays a crucial role in the regulation of gene expression.
