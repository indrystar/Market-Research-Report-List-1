<p><p>Feed pigments are additives added to animal feed to enhance the color of the animal products such as meat, eggs, and milk. Carotenoids, which include astaxanthin and canthaxanthin, are a type of feed pigment derived from plants and microorganisms, contributing to the yellow to red colors. Curcumin, a yellow pigment extracted from turmeric, is also used. Caramel, produced by heating carbohydrates, serves as a brown feed pigment. Spirulina, a blue-green algae, is another feed pigment used for its blue and green shades. Other feed pigments encompass various natural and synthetic options to create desired color effects.</p></p>
