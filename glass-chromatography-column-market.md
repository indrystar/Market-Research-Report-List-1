<p><p>A glass chromatography column is a cylindrical piece of laboratory equipment that is used to separate and purify chemical compounds in a process known as chromatography. It typically consists of a glass tube containing a stationary phase material, such as silica or resin, and a mobile phase liquid that carries the sample being analyzed through the column. The mobile phase and the stationary phase work together to separate the different components of the sample based on their chemical properties.</p><p>The glass chromatography column market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for chromatography in various industries such as pharmaceutical, biotechnology, food and beverage, environmental testing, and forensics.
