<p><p>Genetic testing services refer to analyses performed on human genetic material, such as DNA, to identify variations or mutations that may contribute to the development of a disease or determine an individual's risk of developing certain conditions. These tests provide valuable insights into an individual's genetic makeup and can help in making informed healthcare decisions.</p><p>The genetic testing services market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. Technological advancements in sequencing technologies have made genetic testing more accessible and affordable, resulting in increased demand for these services. Additionally, the growing prevalence of genetic disorders, such as cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and genetic abnormalities in newborns, is driving the market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a shift from traditional genetic testing methods to non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) and direct-to-consumer (DTC) genetic testing. NIPT offers accurate and early detection of chromosomal abnormalities in fetuses, while DTC genetic testing allows consumers to directly access and interpret their genetic information without the need for healthcare professionals. These trends are fueling the growth of the genetic testing services market.</p><p>Moreover, the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technologies into genetic testing services is further augmenting market growth. AI and ML algorithms can analyze large volumes of genetic data quickly and accurately, enabling healthcare providers to personalize treatments and preventive measures based on an individual's genetic profile.</p><p>Overall, with the increasing adoption of advanced sequencing technologies, rising prevalence of genetic disorders, and the integration of AI and ML technologies, the genetic testing services market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.2% during the forecast period.</p></p>

Newborn screening aims to identify genetic disorders in newborns, allowing for early intervention and treatment.
