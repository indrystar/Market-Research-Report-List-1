<p><p>Business-to-business e-commerce market types can be classified into three categories: supplier-oriented, buyer-oriented, and intermediary-oriented markets. In a supplier-oriented market, suppliers have greater control by offering their products or services directly to buyers without the involvement of intermediaries. A buyer-oriented market, on the other hand, gives buyers more control as they actively seek out suppliers and request bids for their specific requirements. In an intermediary-oriented market, intermediaries play a crucial role by bringing suppliers and buyers together, acting as facilitators and providing value-added services to both parties. These market types reflect different levels of control and involvement of suppliers, buyers, and intermediaries.</p></p>
