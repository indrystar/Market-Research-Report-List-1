<p><p>The general aviation headset market can be classified into two main types: wired and wireless. Wired headsets rely on physical connections, typically using cables, to transmit and receive audio signals. They are often preferred for their reliability and consistent audio quality. On the other hand, wireless headsets use radio frequency or Bluetooth technology to establish a wireless connection with the audio source. They offer greater mobility and convenience, allowing users to move freely without being restricted by cables. Both types cater to the needs of general aviation pilots, but they differ in terms of connectivity.</p></p>
