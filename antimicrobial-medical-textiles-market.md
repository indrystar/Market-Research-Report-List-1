<p><p>The antimicrobial medical textiles market includes two major types: non-woven fabrics and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are manufactured by bonding or interlocking fibers together using mechanical, thermal, or chemical methods. They offer benefits such as high absorbency, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for medical applications like wound dressings and surgical gowns. On the other hand, woven fabrics are made by weaving threads in an interlocking pattern. They are durable and provide excellent strength, making them suitable for medical products like bed linens, upholstery, and clothing.</p></p>
