<p><p>Non-contacting thermal mass flow meters are devices used to measure the flow rate of fluids. The thermal dispersion type works by measuring the heat transfer between a heated sensor and the fluid, while the boundary layer type relies on the heat transfer within a boundary layer of fluid near the sensor. The thermal dispersion type is suitable for measuring low flow rates and is ideal for clean fluids, while the boundary layer type is more suitable for higher flow rates and can handle dirty fluids with impurities or contaminants.</p></p>
