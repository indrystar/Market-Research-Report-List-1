<p><p>A cavity drainage system is a waterproofing method used in construction to manage water penetration in basements or below-ground structures. It consists of a series of drainage channels and a sump pump to collect and redirect water away from the building. The system typically includes a membrane that is installed on the walls and floors to create a cavity, allowing any water that enters the structure to be channeled to the drainage system.</p><p>The global cavity drainage system market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The growing construction industry, particularly in developing countries, is a major driver for market growth. Increasing urbanization, population growth, and rising disposable income levels are driving the demand for residential buildings with basements, thereby increasing the need for effective waterproofing solutions like cavity drainage systems.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing awareness about the advantages of cavity drainage systems, such as their ability to prevent water damage and mold growth, is also contributing to market growth. These systems offer long-term protection, durability, and ease of maintenance, which further boosts their adoption in the construction industry.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in cavity drainage systems, such as the development of advanced membranes and improved sump pump technologies, are expected to drive market growth. <p><p>The Cavity Drainage System Market is categorized into three types: Type A Protective Structure, Type B Protective Structure, and Type C Protective Structure. Type A involves a waterproof barrier applied to the external surface, preventing water ingress. Type B uses a waterproofing material on the inner surface, diverting water away from the building. Type C employs a cavity between an inner and outer wall to allow water to collect and drain away through a dedicated system. These systems aim to protect structures from water damage and ensure a dry environment.</p></p>
