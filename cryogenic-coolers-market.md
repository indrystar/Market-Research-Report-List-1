<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The cryogenic coolers market encompasses various types of cooling systems used to achieve low temperatures. Gifford-McMahon cryocoolers employ a piston-like mechanism to compress and expand gases for cooling. Pulse-tube cryocoolers utilize gas oscillations generated by compressed gas to cool down the system. Stirling cryocoolers rely on the displacement of gas in a closed loop system to achieve cooling. Joule-Thomson cryocoolers exploit the temperature drop that occurs when a high-pressure gas expands through a small orifice. Brayton cryocoolers utilize a thermodynamic cycle to achieve cooling. Additionally, there are other types of cryocoolers available in the market with their own unique cooling mechanisms.</p></p>
