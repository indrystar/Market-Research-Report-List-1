<p><p>The collagen sponge market includes two main types: those sourced from cattle and those sourced from pigs. Cattle-derived collagen sponges are made from the connective tissue of cattle, typically the hide or bones. They are known for their high purity and stability. On the other hand, pig-derived collagen sponges are obtained from the connective tissue of pigs, usually their skin or tendons. These sponges offer good biocompatibility and cell adhesion properties. Both types hold significant potential in medical applications like wound healing and tissue engineering.</p></p>
