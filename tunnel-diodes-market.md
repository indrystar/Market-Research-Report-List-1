<p><p>Tunnel diodes are categorized into two main types based on their doping levels. Heavily doped N-type tunnel diodes have a high concentration of negatively charged carriers (electrons) within their structure, allowing for efficient electron flow and low resistance. On the other hand, heavily doped P-type tunnel diodes contain a high concentration of positively charged carriers (holes), resulting in efficient hole flow and low resistance. These market types are crucial as they determine the applications and specific functionality of tunnel diodes in various electronic devices and systems.</p></p>
