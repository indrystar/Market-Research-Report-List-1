<p><p>Animal milk replacers are products designed to replace or supplement the milk produced by a mother animal for their offspring. There are two main types of milk replacers in the market: whey-based and skim-based. Whey-based milk replacers are derived from the liquid separated during cheese-making, which contains high levels of protein, lactose, and minerals. Skim-based milk replacers, on the other hand, are made from skim milk, where the cream has been removed, resulting in a lower fat content. Both types serve as suitable sources of nutrition for young animals unable to consume milk from their mothers.</p></p>
