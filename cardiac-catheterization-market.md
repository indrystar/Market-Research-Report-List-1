<p><p>Cardiac catheterization is a diagnostic procedure performed to gather information about the heart and its blood vessels. It involves inserting a catheter into a large blood vessel, typically in the arm or leg, and guiding it to the heart. This allows the medical professional to measure blood pressure, take blood samples, view the coronary arteries, and conduct various other tests to assess the heart's condition.</p><p>The cardiac catheterization market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a steady rate. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, such as coronary artery disease and heart failure, is one of the key factors driving market growth. Additionally, the growing geriatric population and unhealthy lifestyle choices contribute to the rising incidence of cardiac conditions, thereby fueling the demand for cardiac catheterization procedures.</p><p>Technological advancements in imaging systems, such as angiography systems, have further propelled market growth. <p><p>The cardiac catheterization market comprises various types of catheters used in diagnosing and treating heart conditions. Angiography catheters are used for injecting a contrast dye and visualizing blood vessels. Guiding catheters navigate through the arteries to access certain parts of the heart. Pulmonary artery catheters measure pressures in the heart and lungs. PTCA balloon catheters help in treating blocked arteries by inflating a balloon to open them. Intravascular ultrasound catheters provide real-time imaging of the blood vessels. Electrophysiology catheters are used for diagnosing and treating abnormal heart rhythms.</p></p>
