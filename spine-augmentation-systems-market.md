<p><p>Spine augmentation systems such as percutaneous kyphoplasty and percutaneous vertebroplasty are used for treating spinal fractures or deformities. Percutaneous kyphoplasty involves inserting inflatable balloons into the vertebrae to create space and then injecting bone cement to stabilize the fracture. Percutaneous vertebroplasty involves directly injecting bone cement into the fractured vertebrae. Both procedures aim to reduce pain, restore vertebral height, and improve the quality of life for patients suffering from spinal fractures or deformities. These treatments are minimally invasive, providing a quicker recovery and less postoperative discomfort for patients.</p></p>
