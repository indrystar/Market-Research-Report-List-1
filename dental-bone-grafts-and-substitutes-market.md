<p><p>Dental bone grafts and substitutes play a vital role in dental implant procedures by replacing missing bone tissue. Demineralized bone matrix, xenograft, and synthetic grafts are the main types in the market. Demineralized bone matrix is derived from human bone tissue, often combined with proteins for enhanced healing. Xenografts use bone tissue from animals, typically from cows. Synthetic grafts, on the other hand, are man-made materials that mimic natural bone properties. Each type has its own advantages and considerations, allowing dentists to choose the most suitable option for their patients' needs.</p></p>
