<p><p>Enhanced Geothermal System (EGS) is a type of geothermal energy extraction technology that involves creating an artificial reservoir by fracturing hot rocks deep below the Earth's surface. It is a more advanced and efficient approach compared to conventional geothermal systems, as EGS can extract heat from areas where natural geothermal resources are not readily available.</p><p>EGS works by injecting water or other fluids into deep rock formations at high pressures, causing fractures to form. The fluid is then circulated through these fractures, absorbing heat from the rock, and is subsequently extracted to generate electricity or for direct use in heating and cooling applications. This technology has the potential to unlock vast amounts of geothermal resources worldwide and provide clean, renewable energy.</p><p>The Enhanced Geothermal System Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><ul><li>Deep Enhanced Geothermal System</li><li>Shallow Enhanced Geothermal System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Enhanced Geothermal System (EGS) market consists of two main types: Deep EGS and Shallow EGS. Deep EGS refers to geothermal systems located at depths below 3,000 meters, where hot rock formations are fractured and injected with water to extract heat. Shallow EGS, on the other hand, involves harnessing geothermal energy at depths less than 3,000 meters by creating or enhancing existing natural fractures to improve fluid flow and heat extraction. Both market types aim to utilize geothermal energy as a renewable and sustainable source of power.</p></p>
