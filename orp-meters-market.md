<p><p>ORP meters, also known as oxidation-reduction potential meters, are devices used to measure the ability of a solution to oxidize or reduce other substances. There are two types of ORP meters available in the market: benchtop ORP meters and portable ORP meters. </p><p>Benchtop ORP meters are larger, stationary devices typically used in laboratory or industrial settings. They offer precise and accurate measurements, often with additional features such as data logging and advanced calibration options. Benchtop meters are known for their stability and reliability in providing consistent readings.</p><p>On the other hand, portable ORP meters are smaller, handheld devices designed for easy portability and on-the-go measurements. These meters are commonly used in field applications, such as environmental monitoring or water quality testing. Portable ORP meters are lightweight, compact, and offer convenience in terms of mobility, making them ideal for professionals who require mobility and quick measurements.</p><p>Both benchtop and portable ORP meters serve the same purpose of determining the oxidation-reduction potential, but their form factors and functionalities cater to different usage scenarios and user preferences.</p></p>
